Chapter 1 – The First Cat War

The origins of the First Cat War trace back to the discovery of a stray cat later named Skittles. At the time, no formal nation existed, only a loose group of friends, siblings, and neighbors living within the same apartment complex in Hyderabad. Skittles quickly became a shared point of attachment, cared for informally despite parental restrictions. The absence of clear ownership created uncertainty, but no open conflict had yet emerged.

Tensions escalated when a separate group, later referred to in Kaiser records as “the 504s”, asserted ownership over the cat. Their claim was supported by the appearance of a green collar, which they treated as proof of long-standing possession. Language barriers, differing attitudes toward animal care, and mutual distrust amplified misunderstandings. What might have remained a simple disagreement gradually took on the tone of a territorial dispute.

The decisive moment came during what is now known as the Playground Standoff. Arguments over responsibility, feeding practices, and the cat’s safety transformed personal frustration into collective anger. In response, the founding members declared the formation of the Republic of Kaiser, initially conceived as a federal republic. This act marked the transition from informal disagreement to organized resistance, cementing Skittles as both symbol and catalyst of the conflict.
