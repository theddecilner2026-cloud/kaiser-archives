The Government of Kaiser

State Identity

The nation is officially known as the Union of Kaiser. It is a microstate distributed across residential zones of Hyderabad, with a recorded population of approximately 18 citizens. Despite its limited size, Kaiser maintains a structured political and symbolic system.

System of Government

Kaiser originally functioned as a federal democracy, established during the First Cat War. Over time, due to escalating internal and external threats, the system transitioned into an authoritarian dictatorship, centralized under a single leader for efficiency and unity.

Legislative Structure

The government operates with a bicameral symbolic legislature:

  • The House of Cats – representing loyalty, tradition, and protection
  • The House of Dogs – representing defense, alertness, and enforcement

National Symbols

National Anthem: “Rise Kaiser Rise”
Currency: Kaiser Dollars
Independence Day: 28 June (also known as Skittles Day)

Historical Conflicts

Kaiser has faced multiple internal crises, including ideological divisions and brief communist uprisings, during which the national flag was temporarily altered. These periods were short-lived but contributed to the strengthening of centralized authority.
