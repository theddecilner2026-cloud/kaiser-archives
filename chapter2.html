Chapter 2 – The Collar Crisis

Following the establishment of the Republic of Kaiser, attention shifted to the green collar worn by Skittles. The collar, whose origin was uncertain, became a powerful symbol of ownership and authority. While some believed it belonged to nearby residents, others saw it as a deliberate attempt by the opposing group to legitimize their claim over the cat.

The decision to remove the collar marked a revolutionary act. What began as a simple attempt to resolve confusion was interpreted as provocation. The collar was not returned peacefully; instead, it was discarded during a moment of overexcitement, later recovered from bushes. When representatives of the opposing group arrived at the Kaiser household demanding its return, the encounter carried an air of interrogation rather than negotiation.

The crisis reached its climax when responsibility for the collar’s disappearance was disputed internally, leading to emotional fallout and the involvement of elders. Ultimately, the collar was recovered and reclaimed by the opposing group, who spoke of it as if it were a priceless heirloom. Though the collar itself appeared ordinary, the incident permanently altered relations and confirmed that peaceful resolution was no longer possible.
